Who Owns This?

"Copyright law meets the digital world."

Everything you see online was created by someone. Music, photos, videos, writing, code — all of it is protected by copyright law the moment it is created. Copyright gives creators the exclusive right to copy, distribute, and profit from their work.

But digital technology made copying nearly effortless and free. Some acts are legal. Some are not. Many exist in a grey zone.

As a Digital Rights Consultant, you will review copyright cases and issue rulings — then compare your judgment to the law.

Consultant Briefing

4 Ruling Types

✅ FAIR USE

The use is legal without permission. It serves a transformative, educational, or commentary purpose.

🔵 LICENSED

The creator granted permission via a specific license (Creative Commons, etc.).

🚫 PIRACY

Unauthorized use that violates the creator's exclusive rights.

⚠️ UNCLEAR

The case exists in a legal grey zone with no clear precedent yet.

4 Key Principles

⚙️ Transformative Use

Does the new work add something new — commentary, parody, or meaning?

📏 Proportion

How much of the original was used? A tiny snippet or the whole thing?

💰 Market Harm

Does the use harm the creator's ability to sell their work?

🎓 Educational Purpose

Nonprofit research or teaching is given more legal protection.
