What is a Vector?

In computer science and machine learning, a vector isn't just an arrow in physics class. It's an ordered list of numbers.

These numbers can represent a position in space, the pixels comprising an image, or even a profile of your entertainment preferences.

AI Context: Navigation

Autonomous systems (like delivery drones) don't see "streets"—they rely on vectors. A spatial vector like [x, y] commands a drone to navigate exactly x units horizontally and y units vertically from its origin.

AI Context: Computer Vision

Facial recognition and medical imaging models analyze massive grids of numerical data. By transforming an image into a vector (a list of pixel intensities), algorithms can mathematically compute the similarity between shapes and detect specific patterns.
