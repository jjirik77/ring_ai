What is a Vector?

In computer science and machine learning, a vector isn't just an arrow in physics class.

It's an ordered list of numbers. These numbers can represent a position on a map, the pixels in an image, or even your favorite movie genres.

AI Context: Navigation & Robotics

Self-driving cars and drones don't see "streets"—they see vectors. A vector like [x, y, z] tells a drone exactly where to hover. Robots use vectors to calculate the precise angle and distance to move their mechanical arms.

Vectors as Position

Vectors describe movement or location. A vector like [2, 1] means:
Move 2 steps right (x) and 1 step up (y).

AI Context: Computer Vision

This is how FaceID and medical scanners work. An AI doesn't see a "face" or an "X-ray"—it analyzes a massive grid of pixel values. By comparing these number lists (vectors), it detects patterns like edges, shapes, and tumors.
