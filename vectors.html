What is a Vector?

In computer science and machine learning, a vector isn't just an arrow in physics class. It's an ordered list of numbers.

These numbers can represent a position on a map, the pixels in an image, or even your movie preferences.

AI Context: Navigation

Self-driving cars and drones don't see "streets"—they see vectors. A vector like [x, y, z] tells a drone exactly where to hover relative to its starting point. Robots use vectors to calculate the precise angle and distance to move their mechanical arms.

AI Context: Vision

This is how FaceID and medical scanners work. An AI doesn't see a "face" or an "X-ray"—it analyzes a massive grid of pixel values. By comparing these number lists (vectors), it detects patterns like edges, shapes, and tumors.
