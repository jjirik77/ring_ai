Wildlife Classification Mission

Act as an AI wildlife research analyst in a vital protected zone. Your mission is to build and train an automated classifier program that monitors regional biodiversity, classifying species into three critical groups: Fish, Birds, and Mammals.

Core Machine Learning Concepts

📊
Training Data A set of known, labeled animal records with physical traits used to discover classification patterns.
🧪
Testing Data Completely unseen animal cases used to independently evaluate how accurately our AI model generalizes.
📜
Heuristic Rules Sequential logical constraints (IF trait THEN class) used by the classifier to make automated decisions.
⚖️
Confidence & Bias Confidence tracks system certainty. Overgeneralizing on narrow training sets leads to systemic bias.

📘 Field Trait Guide & AI Heuristics
Feathers: Unique to Birds. Excellent diagnostic filter.
Gills: Unique to Fish. Direct indicator.
Fur: Unique to Mammals. High accuracy.
Live Birth: Highly diagnostic for Mammals.
Beak: Strong for Birds (except Platypus).
Wings & Fins: Multi-class. Bats have wings; cetaceans have fins.
Environment (Aquatic, Land, Warm-blooded, Lays Eggs): Shared ecological traits. Overgeneralizing causes model bias.
