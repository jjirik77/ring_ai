WHAT KIND OF AI IS THIS?

"AI is not one technology. It is a family of seven."

When people say "AI," they usually mean something very specific — but there are actually seven major types of AI, each working in a fundamentally different way.

Machine Learning systems learn patterns. Deep Learning uses layers of neurons. NLP understands language. Vision interprets pixels. Robotics controls physical machines. Expert Systems follow rigid rules. Generative AI creates new content.

In this lab, you will work as an AI Systems Analyst to classify 20 real-world systems across two dimensions: Subset and Class.

ANALYST BRIEFING

Review the classification criteria. This reference will be available throughout the lab.

ML (Machine Learning)

Learns patterns from data without explicit programming.
Ex: Netflix recs, spam filters.

DL (Deep Learning)

Multi-layered neural networks for complex data.
Ex: Face ID, AlphaGo.

NLP (Natural Language)

Understands and generates human speech/text.
Ex: ChatGPT, Autocomplete.

Computer Vision

Analyzes visual info from cameras/images.
Ex: Self-driving lanes, Medical imaging.

AI Robotics

Controls physical machines in the real world.
Ex: Warehouse robots, Surgical arms.

Expert Systems

Uses if-then rules to encode human knowledge.
Ex: Tax software, early chess.

Generative AI

Creates new content (text, image, audio).
Ex: DALL-E, GitHub Copilot.

NARROW AI

One task only. Most AI.

STRONG AI (AGI)

Human-level across all domains.

SUPERINTEL (ASI)

Better than humans at everything.
