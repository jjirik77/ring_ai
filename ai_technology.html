WHAT KIND OF AI IS THIS?

"AI is not one technology. It is a family of seven."

When people use the broad term "AI," they are usually referring to specific subsets of technology. There are actually seven major types of AI, each functioning in a fundamentally different way.

In this simulation, you are an AI Systems Analyst. Your task is to review 20 real-world applications and classify them by their underlying Subset and their Class (scope of intelligence).

ANALYST BRIEFING

Classification criteria and reference material.

Machine Learning (ML)

Systems that learn patterns from data without being explicitly programmed. Ex: Spam filters.

Deep Learning (DL)

Advanced ML using multi-layered neural networks for complex tasks. Ex: Face ID.

NLP

AI that understands, interprets, and generates human language. Ex: Chatbots.

Computer Vision

AI that interprets visual information from cameras and images. Ex: Google Lens.

AI Robotics

Controls physical machines to perform tasks using sensors. Ex: Warehouse bots.

Expert Systems

Rules-based AI that encodes human expert knowledge. Ex: Tax software.

Generative AI

Creates new content (text, image, audio) based on training data. Ex: ChatGPT.

The Three Classes of AI

Narrow AI: Performs one specific task. All current AI products.
Strong AI (AGI): Human-level reasoning. Theoretical.
ASI: Surpasses human intelligence. Theoretical.
