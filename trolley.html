The Trolley Problem

"A thought experiment. A real engineering problem."

In 1967, philosopher Philippa Foot posed a thought experiment: a runaway trolley is headed toward five people. You can pull a lever and divert it — but it will then kill one person on the other track. Do you pull the lever?


For decades, this was philosophy. Then we built self-driving cars. Today, autonomous vehicles are programmed to make split-second collision decisions before the crash ever happens. These rules are permanent, embedded in the vehicle's code, and applied at scale.


You are the engineer. You will program your vehicle's ethics algorithm, watch it play out, and face a review board.
