The Trolley Problem

In 1967, philosopher Philippa Foot posed a thought experiment: a runaway trolley is headed toward five people. You can pull a lever and divert it — but it will then kill one person on the other track.


Today, autonomous vehicles must be programmed to make these choices before they ever reach the road. As an ethics engineer, you will write the Moral Algorithm that decides who lives and who dies in an unavoidable crash.
